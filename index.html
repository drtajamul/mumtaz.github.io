Current Research Focus

I study extracellular vesicles from gut microbes and milk and their roles in host metabolism, immunity, and early-life development. I combine EV isolation, omics, and functional assays in cells and mice.

Key questions

  • How do microbial EV cargo profiles differ between early gut colonizers such as Bifidobacterium infantis and E. coli strains?
  • How do EVs alter insulin, AMPK, and inflammatory signaling in host tissues?
  • How does EV signaling connect gut microbiota to neural processes such as axonal growth and microglial surveillance?

Ongoing projects

  • EV-driven modulation of metabolic pathways
    Multi-omics mapping of microbial EV cargo and host pathway responses with emphasis on insulin and AMPK signaling.
  • Early-life EVs and brain‚Äìgut links
    Integration of EV sRNA data with pathways tied to synaptic refinement, oligodendrocyte maturation, and microglial activity.
  • EV biodistribution and clearance
    Tracking of EV fate in Cre reporter mice to define entry routes, tissue targeting, and clearance profiles.

Future Research Directions

I plan to build an independent program on microbial and milk EVs as cross-kingdom regulators of host metabolism and immune tone.

Planned themes

  • EV-based metabolic interventions
    Use microbial EVs and engineered EV cargo to tune insulin sensitivity, lipid handling, and inflammatory set points in obesity and diabetes models.
  • EV communication in early-life microbiome assembly
    Define how milk and microbial EVs guide colonizer selection, mucosal immunity, and long-term metabolic risk.
  • Engineered probiotic EVs
    Develop probiotic strains that package defined effectors, such as Cre or metabolic regulators, into EVs for targeted delivery.
  • EVs at neuroimmune interfaces
    Extend gut EV findings into circuits that couple metabolism, microglia, and neural development.
